Houdini/Nuke

I've always wondered how some of the special effects are made such as large oceans in Star Wars, dragon fire in Hobbit or lava in Jurassic World. That's where Houdini was mentioned a lot, as a lot of people claim to be a very hard experience to learn it is indeed a fact. But once you get used to it, the software just keeps on giving and it's rewarding. For a couple of months, I've been doing just that learning, practicing and tweaking a small portion of the software. Creating water simulations, dust storms and sci-fi lasers, texturing and rendering is just the beginning of something special. But if you are using Houdini then there is always another great software for compositing, and that is where Nuke comes in. Nuke is an industry-standard tool for post-production such as compositing and creating appealing visual effects alongside Fusion Studio and After Effects. As a node-based software like Houdini, it's very easy to change, modify and control your scene. Alongside Houdini and Nuke, I like to combine other software such as Adobe Audition and After Effects. Audition for sound editing and After Effects for combining whole composition with the sound and rendering. Here is a little preview of my projects in Houdini and Nuke. For full experinece visit my Artstation.

Architectural Visualization

Architectural Visualization will always be special for me when it comes to 3D visualization. Because of Archviz, I fell in love with the world of 3D, and most importantly visualization. My dad at the time was working on an interior project for a local coffee shop and that's when it clicked for me. As much as I love interior work exterior is the one that inspired me to create more and more innovative projects which sometimes can be challenging. All of my Archviz projects are made in 3ds Max and post-produced in Adobe Photoshop. But there is always room for improvement, and there is when Unreal Engine kicks in. The future of Archviz is in the Unreal Engine's hands, and that's why I'm still learning and improving in this field of work. Some of the work is still in progress but will come out very very soon. All of my projects are on my Artstation page.
